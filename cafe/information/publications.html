Super Dollfie Photo Album Another Yourself 2006 ðŸŽ©

2 books (encyclopedia and photobook) in slipcase. Books, 2 disc DVD set, Tokyo Boys Story postcards, and 2 trading cards (Sakaki and Sakaki Awakening) encased in a cardboard box. Very large set at 40x28 cm (full set in cardboard box). Includes English translations for everything.

Super Dollfie Encyclopedia 2006 includes basic information on super dollfie, all releases up to the publication, SD specific terms, basic background stories for the story arcs, info on omukae, Tenshi no Sumika, ordering the 5 types (Standard, FCS, Limited, One-off, Auction), customization information, details about Tenshi no Sato Kachu-an, interviews, chronological list of releases.

The background for the story arcs covers Oath of Silver Coin, The Maidens' Promise, A Quiet Banquet, Harajuku Memories, and Tokyo Boys Story. Tokyo Boys Story is by far the most developed storyline and short stories between the characters have been written.

Interviews included are with Shogo Kariyazaki, Akihiro Enku (includes a photo of his prototype SD for his wife), Kitamura, Ohashi.

The photobook includes a couple photos of Seirei Sakaki Awakening, photos of the Chapter 1 Tokyo Boys Story (Shiro, Tsukasa, Isao, Yukinoko, Jun), The Maiden's Promise (Emma, Liz), Oath of Silver Coin (Captain and Scarface Cecile). There is text in both Japanese and English accompanying the characters in their story arcs. I do not recognize any of these photos as having been shown in promotional photos for these dolls.

DVD contents: To be watched. DVD is NTSC Region All.

Mooks and Magazines

Dolly Dolly is published by Graphic-sha. Last issue (39) was published in 2019.

Dollybird is published by Hobby Japan. Some dolls have been exclusively sold through this magazine. This magazine is still publishing.

Dolly Japan is also published by Hobby Japan. There are 5 volumes (0-4). Last issue published in 2015.

U-noa Freak is also published by Hobby Japan. It includes fanmade patterns and tutorials for Unoa dolls. Three volumes were published.

Western magazines that featured BJD included Haute Doll, Dolls, Dollreader, and Fashion Doll Quarterly (FDQ). The main focus was rarely BJD, but they did appear. Both magazines have ceased publication.
