My Personal Ideal Doll Traits

If you want me to buy your doll just follow this guide to get to my heart. I don't think anyone can get upset over this list!

  • 1/3 scale

    • There's something so impressive about large dolls. It's my favorite size by far. I will only make exceptions for Kinoko Juice.

  • Flat chested

    • Young girls or mature girls doesn't matter! The less titty the better. Cute!

  • Open mouth smile

    • MY HEART. I love little simplistic white teeth, but not detailed ones. I really don't like when teeth are painted with a line between them.

  • White or normal skin

    • Let's face it, I only bought tan dolls because they were limited. I used to not care for WS, but in 10 years the doll will be almost NS anyway.

  • Human ears

    • My elf ear days are over. No fantasy parts please. Goodbye.

  • Very specific knees

    • When the resin from the thigh piece extends over the kneecap rather than the resin from the calf piece. i.e. every unoa and all DDs past DD1.

  • Single jointed

    • No fancy knees, no fancy elbows, and just one joint around the waist area is perfectly fine.

  • One piece torso

    • It's seriously so pretty. Mobility is severly compromised, but it's worth it to me for a well sculpted torso.

  • Multiple option hands

    • I want them all!!!!

Doll Traits I HATE

Just because I don't like them doesn't mean you shouldn't (I guess). These are all dealbreakers for me.

  • Ugly knees

    • Knee bean joints drive me insane and most double jointed knees are done in this way. If the knee bean can turn around in the socket, I will be even more upset. Fairyland's knee mechanism doesn't bother me.

  • Double-ball hip joints

    • i.e. Atelier Momoni. Also hip joints where the ball is attached to the torso i.e. Dust of Dolls. ALSO mobility joints.

  • Rigid sculpting
  • Excessively long arms

    • i.e. Angel Fantasy. Slenderman.

  • Flat hands and long wrists
  • Noodle limbs

    • I do want my dolls to be thin, but I dont want them to be shapeless!! Danny has this problem big time.

  • Upper arm mobility joints

    • i.e. Bluefairy's neo bodies. This makes your design look like a playline doll faster than any other choice.

  • Domuya flexi bodies

    • I'm glad this went out of style because I hate everything about them. The cut outs in the joints absolutely disgust me.

  • B&G Sapphira

    • I cant put into words why I hate this doll so much. It wouldnt make sense even if I could explain it.

  • Long wait times

    • I will never EVER wait over 6 months for a doll again. I don't care how much I want it.

  • Extreme pear shape

    • I think very few companies are able to pull this off. I like Kinoko Juice and some of Enaibi's shapes, but I've come to greatly dislike the current trend of gigantic doll *sses.

  • British accents

    • If my doll has a british accent, we have a big problem.

wait times

Unfortunately, most doll companies run off the preorder model, where you pay in full and wait while your doll is produced.

This is a terrible business model but most of us are willing to put up with it since these are small businesses and there's not really any other choice rather than buying used.

Do not let an untrusted company in another country to hold your money outside of paypal's protection period. You probably should not ordering from companies with wait times over 6 months.

Back in 2010, Customhouse had a 1 year wait time, which was considered outrageous at the time. Somehow a 1 year wait is slowly becoming the norm in the 2020s.

choosing a doll

Choosing a doll to fit your budget usually ends in selling the doll at a loss and possibly deciding this hobby isn't for you.

You may not find the perfect doll until you've viewed hundreds of dolls. You will know when you're in love. "This one is good" isn't good enough. Plenty of dolls are good. You want to buy the BEST.

Starter dolls are cheap for a reason. Keep in mind, you would still be spending a significant amount of money that could be put toward something you'd love much more.

After buying and opening a doll that I once considered to be a pipe dream, I could not recommend saving up more. It's worth it and I hope everyone can experience this. The feeling of opening the box of a dream doll is insanely different than opening an impulse buy.

Chinese doll companies

Almost all of the big Chinese doll companies make dolls not worth looking at. They look like they paid a sculptor a few bucks to pop out a head and another a few more bucks to pop out a body. Not many were started by artists who hoped to share their work with the world. Anything considered a "starter" doll is not worth buying. You will most likely find that the worthwhile Chinese dolls cost the same as Korean and Japanese dolls.

There are many smaller Chinese artists who are passionate and deserve your attention. These dolls are definitely not mass produced with the sole intention of making money. These dolls will not cost you $200 for an mini, so don't go in with that expectation.

Myoudoll can manage to sculpt a cute face and cover it with a nice faceup but their bodies are as basic as the other cheap companies. It's been shown the faceups they ship out aren't the same quality as the promo pictures.
